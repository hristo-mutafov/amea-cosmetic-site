Operations or diseases can cause cosmetic defects. This is where the necessity for medical micropigmentation begins.

What is camouflage technique?

It is a reliable and safe method for covering scars. Camouflage is a term that encompasses various micropigmentation techniques designed to reduce the visibility of scars, stretch marks, and hyperpigmentation. It blends scars with the surrounding skin using permanent makeup pigments that the body naturally breaks down over time.

Practitioners reconstruct the areolas of breast cancer patients, giving them at least a visual aspect of normalcy. Medical micropigmentation is also used for hyperpigmentation or to correct vitiligo.

THE PROCEDURE CAN BE APPLIED TO:

  • Scars after tattoo removal

  • Scars after trauma and injuries

  • Scars after surgeries

  • Scars after plastic surgeries such as abdominoplasty, facelift, rhinoplasty, and anchor-type scars after breast implant placement.

  • Restoring the color of areolas

  • Consequences of animal bites

  • Stretch marks

  • Burns

  • Vitiligo
